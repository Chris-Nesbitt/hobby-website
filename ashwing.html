The Ashwing

And unto the umbral world descended the great Ashwing, whose feathers were of fire and ash. For in it were bound the two great truths: the inevitability of the end and the promise of new beginnings.

And It beheld the world in darkness and in stillness. The forests slept, and the seeds beneath the soil lived in endless slumber. So, It took its own burning heart and rent it in two. One half, the Sun, It cast into the heavens, so that all living things might know the hour of rising.

The other half It kept within its breast, the flames of both life and death. And when the forests grew old and heavy with their own shadow, the Ashwing descended in a storm of embers. Yet when the flames died, the earth breathed again and green shoots pierced the ashen soil, and life returned anew.

-Creations 2:1
