Introduction

If you find yourself, for whatever reason, wanting to leave the safety of The Veil, its important that you are aware of the risks that come from being outside the barrier. The most prevalent of these risks are spirits. These spirits can range anywhere from celestial beings to a resurrection spell gone wrong.

While not all of these spirits are out to get you, its important to know what to ignore, what to fight, what to seek assistance from, and what to turn tail and sprint from. I have travelled far and wide to get as much information on these spirits as I can, be it from my own experience, written logs or the stories of those affected, and put it all in one place. The results of my research are contained here, in a portable guidebook for daring adventurers such as yourself.

Now I imagine those who aren't interested in the exact workings of spirits have long since abandoned this page, but for those who are more curious, I shall give you some basic information that I have learned in my travels.

All spirits are classified into 4 major groups based on their creation:

  1. Original Spirits - spirits who have existed since, or even before, the creation of Hallowmere

  2. Carnivorous Spirits - spirits who became a spirit after consuming another spirit

  3. Hereditary Spirits - spirits who are created by another spirit (oftentimes through birth)

  4. Persistent Spirits - spirits who are the remnants of a dead creature, whose soul remained to fulfill a purpose

These classifications serve to provide a rough basis upon which to choose your actions. Original and Hereditary spirits, for example, tend to ignore the average traveller, for you are but a speck in their long lifespan. On the other hand, Carnivorous spirits, as is to be expected, will often behave more hostile. Persistent spirits are less clear, as their intentions are based on whatever purpose they have and differs on a case by case basis.

I intend to regularly update this book to ensure it is up to date on all the spirits we find. If you see a spirit not mentioned here, please contact me here.

If you so desire, I would suggest the work of a colleague of mine, Judika Illes, to get a better understanding of the topic.

If you have taken the time to read this introduction, I appreciate your indulgance of my rambling. I do hope you enjoy the guidebook and that it offers you some assistance in your travels.

-Alaric Thorne
